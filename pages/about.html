About Voice Diary

Speak, and it's written.

Voice Diary is a simple, private journaling app built for people who want to capture their thoughts quickly — whether that means typing them out or just speaking out loud and letting the app write it down for you.

The idea is simple: a diary shouldn't get in the way of the moment you're trying to remember. Some days you have time to sit and write. Other days, a two-minute voice note on your commute is all you'll manage. Voice Diary tries to support both without friction.

What you can do

  • Write diary entries the traditional way, with a clean, distraction-free editor.
  • Record voice notes and have them saved alongside your written entries.
  • Decorate entries with stickers and a personal touch.
  • Lock your diary with a PIN for privacy.
  • Export entries — including as a formatted PDF — to keep for yourself.
  • Use the app offline; your entries live on your own device.

Why it's built this way

A diary is personal. That's why Voice Diary keeps your entries stored locally on your device rather than requiring an account or a cloud server by default. You're free to export and back up your own data whenever you'd like.

Who's behind it

Voice Diary is an independent project built and maintained by a solo developer who wanted a lightweight, honest journaling tool without unnecessary bloat. Feedback and feature ideas are always welcome — see the Contact page to get in touch.
